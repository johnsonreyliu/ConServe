Conserve Water

Turn off the faucet while brushing your teeth. You can save 8 gallons a day according to the U.S. EPA.

Fix leaky faucets as soon as you can.

Install water-saving faucets, toilets, and shower-heads in your home.

Take shorter showers

Turn off your sprinklers during rainy seasons.

Water your lawn and garden early in the morning when it’s cool out - you reduce the amount of water that evaporates

Recycle Waste

Keep a separate trash can for your recyclables. It makes trash day easier!

Plant an herb garden at home.

Bring your own reusable bags to the grocery store.

Use a reusable water bottle - you’ll save money, and reduce waste.

Use old rags or t-shirts instead of paper towels to clean your house.

Go paperless and pay your bills online.

 

Save Electricity

Switch all of your lightbulbs to energy-efficient ones. CFL bulbs will get you the greatest savings.

Look for the ‘energy star’ label when purchasing appliances.

Switch off your lights when you leave the room or leave the house.

Unplug unused chargers and appliances.

Air conditioner maintenance will save money and ensure peak efficiency

If you have outdoor lights, swap them for solar energy powered lights
